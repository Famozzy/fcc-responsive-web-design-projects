Tribute Page for
B.J. Habibie

1936 - 2019

Prof. Dr. Ing. H.
Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie

was an Indonesian engineer and politician who was the third president of Indonesia from 1998 to 1999. Less than three months after his inauguration as the seventh vice president in March 1998, he succeeded Suharto who resigned after 31 years in office.

B.J. Habibie's Timeline

  • 1936

    Born in Parepare, South Sulawesi

  • 1360

    Received an engineer's degree in Germany with the title Diplom-Ingenieur

  • 1962

    Returned to Indonesia for three months on sick leave, married with Hasri Ainun, then returning to Germany

  • 1963

    Had a son, Ilham Akbar Habibie

  • 1965

    Delivered his dissertation in aerospace engineering and received the grade of "very good," giving him the title Doktoringenieur (Dr.-Ing.)

  • 1974

    He was promoted to vice president of the company he worked, Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm in Hamburg

  • 1978

    He was appointed as State Minister of Research and Technology (Indonesian: Menteri Negara Riset dan Teknologi, Menristek)

  • 1990

    While serving in cabinet, Habibie was also elected as the first Chair of the Indonesian Association of Muslim Intellectuals (ICMI) in 1990. This modernist Muslim organization provided him with a political base, linked to but independent of the Suharto administration

  • 1992

    Suharto appointed him as deputy daily coordinator for the chairman of the executive board

  • 1998

    Habibie was elected as Vice President just two months before succeded previous president as the Constitution says

  • 2006

    He released a book called Detik-Detik Yang Menentukan: Jalan Panjang Indonesia Menuju Demokrasi (Decisive Moments: Indonesia's Long Road Towards Democracy). The book recalled the events of May 1998 which led to his rise to the Presidency

  • 2019

    He was admitted to Gatot Soebroto Army Hospital, where he was undergoing treatments for heart problems, namely cardiomyopathy and died on 11 September 2019.
